The presence of ventricular tachycardia in patients with congestive heart failure increases the probability of dying; in class III or IV heart failure, the presence of unsustained ventricular tachycardia on a 24 hr continuous ECG recording increases the odds of dying about threefold over a 1 to 2 year follow-up period.
