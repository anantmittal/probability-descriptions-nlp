Miscarriage risk after 12 weeks
The majority of miscarriages do take place in the first twelve weeks of pregnancy. The medical term is “early miscarriage,” and the cause is usually chromosomal abnormalities in the fetus. It's far less likely — but still possible — for a pregnancy to fail after those first three months.
